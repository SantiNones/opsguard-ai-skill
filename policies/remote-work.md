# Remote Work Policy

**Policy ID:** RW  
**Version:** 2024.3  
**Effective Date:** March 1, 2024  
**Owner:** HR Business Partners + Legal  
**Review Cycle:** Semi-annual (rapidly evolving area)

---

## Purpose

Define remote work eligibility, approval procedures, and restrictions—especially for cross-border arrangements—to ensure compliance with tax and labor laws.

---

## Rules

### RW-01: Domestic Remote Work

**Rule:** Employees may work remotely within their country of employment with manager approval.

**Requirements:**
- Permanent remote: Requires HRBP approval
- Temporary remote (<30 days): Manager approval only
- Must maintain local address on file
- Must be available during core hours (9am-3pm local time)

---

### RW-02: Cross-Border Remote Work

**Rule:** Working from a different country requires pre-approval from HRBP and Legal.

**Rationale:** Cross-border work triggers:
- Tax implications (personal income tax, corporate nexus)
- Labor law compliance (local employment laws)
- Data privacy regulations (GDPR, etc.)
- Payroll entity restrictions

**Approval Process:**
1. Employee submits request (min 30 days advance)
2. Manager initial approval
3. HRBP reviews business justification
4. Legal reviews compliance implications
5. Payroll confirms tax/payroll feasibility
6. Decision within 10 business days

**Duration Limits:**
- Emergency situations: Up to 5 days with post-hoc approval
- Planned work: Up to 30 days per calendar year
- Extended arrangements: Requires contract amendment

---

### RW-03: Equipment and Security

**Rule:** Remote employees must maintain security standards.

**Requirements:**
- VPN required for all work activity
- Company laptop (no personal devices for work)
- Secure home network (no public WiFi without VPN)
- Lock screens when unattended

---

### RW-04: Time Zone Coordination

**Rule:** Remote employees must coordinate with their team's core hours.

**Flexibility:**
- Can work non-standard hours with manager approval
- Must attend required meetings (synchronous)
- Response time: Within 4 hours during business days

---

## Examples

### Domestic Remote (Approved)
> Employee in SF works from LA for 2 weeks. Manager approves. No compliance issues.

### Cross-Border (Approved with Conditions)
> Employee works from Canada for 3 weeks. HRBP and Legal approve. Payroll adjusts tax withholdings. Employee signs tax acknowledgment.

### Cross-Border (Denied)
> Employee wants to work from Thailand for 2 months. Denied—exceeds 30-day limit and creates tax nexus issues.

### Emergency Cross-Border (Post-hoc)
> Employee stranded in Mexico due to flight cancellation. Works 3 days. Submits post-hoc emergency approval. Approved as exceptional circumstance.
