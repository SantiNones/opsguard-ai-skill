# Vacation Leave Policy

**Policy ID:** VL  
**Version:** 2024.1  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Owner:** HR Operations  
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## Purpose

Define vacation entitlements, carryover rules, and leave request procedures to ensure work-life balance while maintaining operational coverage.

---

## Rules

### VL-01: Vacation Carryover

**Rule:** Up to 5 unused vacation days may be carried over per calendar year.

**Details:**
- Maximum carryover: 5 days
- Carried days expire March 31 of following year
- Cannot cash out carried days
- Pro-rated for mid-year hires

**Calculation:**
```
Carryover = min(Unused Days, 5)
```

---

### VL-02: Leave Approval

**Rule:** Vacation requests require manager approval with 2 weeks notice.

**Exceptions (1 week notice allowed):**
- Family emergencies
- Medical appointments
- Pre-approved events (weddings, etc.)

**Denial Reasons (Valid):**
- Insufficient coverage during peak period
- Employee has negative balance
- Conflicts with blackout dates (sales kickoffs, etc.)

---

### VL-03: Leave Balance

**Rule:** Employees cannot take vacation with negative balance.

**Advance Policy:**
- New hires: Can take vacation before accruing (up to 5 days)
- Negative balance must be repaid if employee leaves

---

### VL-04: PTO vs Vacation

**Rule:** This policy applies only to vacation days. PTO (if offered) follows separate PTO policy.

**In Regions with PTO:**
- No separate vacation tracking
- PTO supersedes this policy

---

## Examples

### Standard Carryover
> Employee has 7 unused days on Dec 31.  
> 5 days carry over to next year.  
> 2 days forfeited.

### Carryover Expiration
> Employee carried 5 days from 2023.  
> Must use by March 31, 2024.  
> Unused days forfeited April 1.

### Leave Request Approved
> Employee requests 3 days with 3 weeks notice. Manager approves. Coverage adequate.

### Leave Request Denied
> Employee requests 5 days during board meeting week. Blackout date. Manager denies with alternative dates.
