# Time Tracking Policy

**Policy ID:** TT  
**Version:** 2024.1  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Owner:** Payroll Operations  
**Review Cycle:** Annual

---

## Purpose

Ensure accurate time recording for payroll, compliance, and workforce management while providing fair mechanisms for corrections when errors occur.

---

## Rules

### TT-01: Missed Clock-Ins

**Rule:** All missed clock-in entries must be reported within 5 business days of the missed date.

**Procedure:**
1. Employee submits time correction request via portal or to manager
2. Manager reviews and approves within 2 business days
3. Payroll processes correction in next pay cycle

**Restriction:** Corrections older than 30 days require Payroll Lead approval.

---

### TT-02: Manager Approval Required

**Rule:** All retroactive time entry modifications require documented manager approval.

**Requirements:**
- Manager must verify the hours worked
- Manager must confirm the reason for the correction
- Approval must be documented in writing (email or system)

**Exception:** System errors (not employee error) can be corrected by Payroll without manager approval, but must be logged.

---

### TT-03: Overtime Corrections

**Rule:** Any time correction involving overtime requires additional Payroll review.

**Rationale:** Overtime has wage implications and compliance requirements under labor law.

**Procedure:**
1. Manager approves correction
2. Payroll reviews overtime calculation
3. Correction flagged for audit trail

---

### TT-04: Audit Trail

**Rule:** All time corrections must be logged with timestamp, original entry, corrected entry, approver, and reason.

**Retention:** 7 years for compliance purposes.

---

### TT-05: Peer Attendance Privacy

**Rule:** Employees cannot access another employee's time entries or attendance records unless they have an authorized HR, Payroll, or manager role for that employee.

**Restriction:** Peer attendance lookups must not return time entry details, attendance exceptions, clock-in records, or payroll-sensitive data.

**Allowed Access:** Managers may view attendance exceptions for their direct reports. HR Operations and Payroll Operations may access records only for authorized operational workflows.

---

### TT-06: Payroll Cutoff Risk

**Rule:** Time corrections submitted near payroll cutoff or after the payroll period has closed require HR Operations or Payroll review before any change is applied.

**Rationale:** Retroactive time changes near payroll cutoff may affect compensation, taxes, overtime calculations, and the payroll audit trail.

**Procedure:** Route urgent cutoff-risk corrections to HR Operations or Payroll Operations before applying any update.

---

## Examples

### Valid Correction
> Employee forgot to clock in on Tuesday. Reported Wednesday. Manager verified and approved. Correction processed in next payroll.

### Invalid Correction (Too Late)
> Employee requests correction for hours worked 45 days ago. Requires Payroll Lead escalation.

### System Error
> Employee clocked in but system recorded wrong time. IT confirmed system bug. Payroll corrected without manager approval per exception.
